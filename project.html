Food Ordering System(Mobile App)

As a vital member of the Food Ordering System team, I played a pivotal role in crafting the Food Menu/Online Ordering App. This initiative aimed to serve a wide array of users, ranging from loyal customers who frequently order take-outs to those who are exploring the Food ordering System for the first time. The app was developed to adapt to the evolving preferences and needs of our customers, who seek greater convenience, variety, and customization in their dining experiences. I'm excited to guide you through the thought process behind the app design that significantly enhanced the Food ordering System experience for all users.

My Role

UX Researcher & Designer

Responsibility

Research, Prototyping, Testing, Designing

Project Duration

March 2024 — June 2024


Pain Points

Inefficient Ordering Experience: The mechanism of placing orders at Food ordering system is marked by inefficiencies, including extended phone calls, repetitive confirmations, and the cumbersome task of monitoring orders.

Absence of Order History: The lack of easily accessible references leaves customers struggling to recall their previous choices, leading to a less-than-optimal experience.

Lack of Rewarding Customer Loyalty: The success of our app heavily relies on its loyal customers. However, the current system lacks a mechanism to acknowledge and reward these dedicated patrons.

Restricted Decision-Making: When placing orders over the phone, we frequently overlook important details such as customer reviews, recommendations, and daily specials.

Findings

Users expressed the necessity for a notification or indication when an item is successfully added to the cart.

Absence of an address section within their profile, which was a detail I had missed out, but a crucial point for me to trigger my eye for errors.

A payment confirmation to be displayed, once the restaurant receives their payment.

Addition of a “Repeat Order” feature, rather than just tracking past orders, to streamline the ordering process.

Few customers expressed interest in an “Order Instructions” section to convey their preferences or instructions for the items and/or delivery.


Lo-FI

Order History

The previous system created an inconvenience for customers who could review their past orders but lacked the ability to reorder them instantly. In response, I introduced a “Repeat Order” feature, streamlining the process, adding convenience, and saving their valuable time. Now, with a simple click, they can effortlessly replicate their favorite orders.

Cart View

The absence of an option for customers to give specific instructions for a dish or the entire order limited the ability to convey their requirement. Addressing this, I implemented a dedicated instructions field, enabling customers to easily communicate their preferences for aspects like saltiness, sweetness, spiciness, and more, ensuring precise alignment with their tastes.

Colors

Chilli Red
The primary color utilized is appropriately named Chilli Red, an ideal choice for a burger website due to its appetizing and attention-grabbing qualities. Given our identity as a burger brand, the use of red is indispensable. This bold and vibrant color can instantly evoke feelings of hunger and excitement, complementing the indulgent and savory nature of burgers.
Neutrals
Black provides a sophisticated and powerful anchor to the palette, especially against a white background, enhancing readability through strong visual contrast. Additionally, it contributes to establishing clear distinctions, effectively delineating lines, borders, and other elements, thereby improving overall clarity and organization on the website.

White serves as an excellent choice due to its versatility and cleanliness. It imparts a sense of simplicity and sophistication. As a neutral backdrop, it enhances readability, and complements other colors effectively, contributing to an overall visually appealing and timeless design.

My Take

The Impact:

Throughout every phase of the design process, I have acquired valuable insights that have deepened my ability to empathize with users. This journey has left a lasting mark on my perspective, enabling me to approach design challenges with a user-centric mindset. I continuously integrated user preferences and experiences, fostering an understanding that now shapes my approach to creating meaningful and impactful designs.

What I learned:

What I’ve really grasped from this project is that iteration is an ongoing journey without a clear endpoint — and that’s where the real excitement lies. Nonetheless, the overarching objective remains to attain a remarkable design, crafting a captivating product that ensures a uniform experience for all.

Accessibility Considerations:

1. Initially, My color palette fell short of the contrast standards set by WCAG. However, with subtle adjustments, I successfully elevated the same.

2. Adhering to W3C guidelines, I precisely calibrated animations with a 300-ms timing focus for seamless app navigation.

3. Augmented the typographical hierarchy for specific elements, boosting readability and visual impact.

Next Steps

Refining the High-Fidelity Prototype

I intend to kickstart a second round of usability studies, aimed at tackling potential pain points, particularly concerning payment methods. These studies are vital to identifying and competently resolving any issues that may arise.

Build a Responsive Web App for FOS

Prioritizing user preferences is crucial, particularly since not everyone favors downloading a mobile app. Therefore, I advocate for developing a responsive web app, expanding D-Lite’s accessibility to a larger and more diverse audience.
